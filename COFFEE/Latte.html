What is a Latte?


A Latte is a coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk.

The term Latte is a shortened form of the Italian caffè latte [kafˌfɛ lˈlatte], which means "milk coffee". The word is also sometimes spelled latté or lattè in English with different kinds of accent marks, which can be a hyperforeignism or a deliberate attempt to indicate that the word is not pronounced according to the rules of English orthography.

In Italy, caffè latte is almost always prepared at home, for breakfast only. The coffee is brewed with a stovetop moka pot and poured into a cup containing heated milk. (Unlike the 'international' latte drink, the milk in the Italian original is generally not foamed, and sugar is added by the drinker, if at all).

How to make:

Ingredients

18g ground espresso (or 1 espresso pod)

250ml milk

You will also need

the right mug/glass, 300-350ml capacity

Method

  1. Have a latte mug/glass ready. Optional: pre-heat the mug by adding water, then boiling it in the microwave.
  2. Pull a 30ml espresso shot using a coffee machine and pour it into the base of the mug/glass.
  3. Steam the milk with the steamer attachment, creating 2-3cm of foam.
  4. Pour in the hot milk, start from 1-2 inches above the cup moving the jug closer to the mug as you pour. Once the milk jug is almost touching the surface of the coffee, tilt the jug to speed up the rate of pour. As you accelerate, the milk will hit the back of the cup and start naturally folding in on itself to create a pattern on the top.
