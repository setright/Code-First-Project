What is a Mocha?


A Mocha is a chocolate-flavored variant of a caffè latte.

The name is derived from the city of Mocha, Yemen, which was one of the centers of early coffee trade.

Like a caffè latte, caffè mocha is based on espresso and hot milk but with added chocolate flavoring and sweetener, typically in the form of cocoa powder and sugar. Many varieties use chocolate syrup instead, and some may contain dark or milk chocolate. Caffè mocha, in its most basic formulation, can also be referred to as hot chocolate with espresso.

How to make:

Ingredients

18g ground espresso (or 1 espresso pod)

250ml milk

1/2 tbsp chocolate syrup or 1 heaped tsp cocoa powder

dollop of whipped cream

To serve

mini marshmellos (optional)

You will also need

the right mug/glass, 300-350ml capacity

Method

  1. Have a large mug, ready. Optional: pre-heat the mug by adding water, then boiling it in the microwave.
  2. Using a coffee machine, pull a 30ml espresso shot into the cup and then mix in the chocolate syrup/powder.
  3. Steam the milk in a jug with the steamer attachment, creating 2-3cm of foam.
  4. Pour in the hot milk, start from 1-2 inches above the mug/glass moving the jug closer to the mug as you pour.
  5. Add the dollop of whipped cream on top, and mini marshmellos for your enjoyment.
