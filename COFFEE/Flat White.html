What is a Flat White?


A Flat White is a coffee drink consisting of espresso with microfoam (steamed milk with small, fine bubbles and a glossy or velvety consistency).

It is comparable to a latte, but smaller in volume and with less microfoam, therefore having a higher proportion of coffee to milk, and milk that is more velvety in consistency – allowing the espresso to dominate the flavour, while being supported by the milk.

It is essentially the opposite of a "dry cappuccino", which has dry frothy foam but no liquid milk. A flat white has milk (or microfoam) but no froth. The milk should be velvety rather than fluffy and therefore stronger, which requires a shorter, ristretto espresso shot to avoid harsh flavours.[9] The flat white is similar to an original Italian cappuccino, which is a single espresso with microfoam served in a 150–160 ml cup.

How to make:

Ingredients

18g ground espresso (or 1 espresso pod)

100ml milk

You will also need

the right cup, 150-200ml capacity

Method

  1. Have a coffee cup ready. Optional: pre-heat the mug by adding water, then boiling it in the microwave.
  2. Using a coffee machine, pull a 30ml espresso shot into the base of the cup.
  3. Steam the milk with the steamer attachment, creating 1-2cm of foam.
  4. Pour in the hot milk, start from 1-2 inches above the cup moving the jug closer to the mug as you pour. Once the milk jug is almost touching the surface of the coffee, tilt the jug to speed up the rate of pour. As you accelerate, the milk will hit the back of the cup and start naturally folding in on itself to create a pattern on the top.
